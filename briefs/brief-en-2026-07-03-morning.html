June jobs report stalls: only 57,000 added, unemployment dips to 4.2%

Trump administration won't renew USMCA in its current form

Six years to the day after the U.S.–Mexico–Canada trade agreement took effect, the administration said it will not extend the roughly $2 trillion pact as written, letting the July 1 review deadline pass. USTR will instead negotiate bilaterally and trilaterally with Mexico and Canada through the joint-review process to press for a revised deal.

Why it matters: It removes one of the last pillars of stability in North American trade, clouding the outlook for autos, agriculture and cross-border supply chains.

Indirect technical talks in Doha wrapped up with Qatari mediators citing "positive progress" on the Islamabad memorandum, covering Iran's frozen $6 billion, its nuclear program and the Strait of Hormuz.
