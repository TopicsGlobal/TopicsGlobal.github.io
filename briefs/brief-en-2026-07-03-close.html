2) Top Stories

Job growth stalls: June payrolls up only 57,000

The economy added 57,000 jobs in June, well below the ~113,000–115,000 consensus, and April–May payrolls were revised down by a combined 74,000. The unemployment rate ticked down to 4.2%, but largely because labor-force participation slid 0.3 point to 61.5% — the lowest since March 2021. Leisure and hospitality shed 61,000 jobs, while professional services, social assistance and health care drove the gains.

Why it matters: The cool report breaks a three-month hot streak and undercuts the case for the Fed's more hawkish officials to push additional rate hikes.

Source: CNBC / Bureau of Labor Statistics

Russia pounds Kyiv in massive strike; Trump–Zelensky talks set for Sunday

Russia launched more than 70 missiles — nearly half ballistic — and almost 500 drones at Ukraine overnight into Thursday, killing at least 27 people in Kyiv and injuring more than 100. Poland scrambled jets and Finland restricted airspace during the barrage, which Moscow cast as retaliation for Ukrainian strikes on its oil sector.

Why it matters: The attack lands days before President Zelensky meets President Trump in Florida on Sunday for talks on ending the nearly four-year war, raising the stakes for U.S. decisions on air-defense supplies.

Source: ABC News / NPR / CNBC

U.S.–Iran talks make "positive progress"; Trump says Tehran has agreed to "just about everything"

Qatar said indirect U.S.–Iran meetings in Doha made positive progress on the 60-day memorandum of understanding signed June 17 after the U.S.–Israeli war with Iran. In a CNBC interview, President Trump said Iran has "agreed to just about everything we need" on nuclear restrictions, though disputes persist — including Iran's demand to charge fees for Strait of Hormuz transit.

Why it matters: Rising oil shipments through Hormuz and de-escalation hopes are pushing crude prices lower, directly easing U.S. inflation pressure.

Source: CNN / CNBC

Tesla posts blowout Q2 deliveries — stock sinks 7.5% anyway

Tesla delivered 480,126 vehicles in Q2, crushing the ~397,000 consensus and topping even the most bullish forecasts, up 25% year over year. The stock nonetheless fell about 7.5% — its worst day in nearly a year — as the result had been priced in after a 12% run-up earlier in the week and investors stayed focused on the AI and self-driving story behind its lofty valuation.

Why it matters: A sell-the-news rout after a record beat shows how much of Tesla's ~200x forward multiple rests on autonomy hopes rather than car sales.

Source: CNBC / Yahoo Finance

Democrats on the House Natural Resources Committee released a 55-page report accusing the Freedom 250 organization of helping turn the nation's semiquincentennial into what they call a "hotbed of corruption and self-enrichment," alleging tactics that could constitute criminal fraud. Dangerous heat threatens July 4th events; D.C. near 102°F

A heat wave is forecast to push Washington temperatures to around 102°F with a heat index up to 113 this weekend, prompting Capitol Police to weigh whether the public can attend the Capitol Fourth concert and fireworks. Organizers of America 250 events across the country face similar decisions.

Why it matters: Extreme heat poses public-safety risks for millions attending outdoor 250th-anniversary celebrations and strains power grids over the holiday weekend.

Source: NPR

After Supreme Court loss on Lisa Cook, Trump keeps pressing to reshape the Fed

The White House is continuing efforts to remove or replace Federal Reserve board members after Monday's 5–4 Supreme Court ruling that President Trump cannot fire Governor Lisa Cook while her legal challenge proceeds. The Court held the Fed stands on unique constitutional footing even as it granted the president broader power over other independent agencies.

Why it matters: The ruling shields the Fed's independence for now — a key anchor for markets — but the administration's pressure campaign on the central bank is not over.

Source: NBC News / CNBC
