The National Weather Service warned of extreme heat risk with heat indices up to 115°F, while evening storms disrupted events in D.C., New York, Philadelphia and Boston.

Iran opens weeklong Khamenei funeral amid fresh Strait of Hormuz warnings

Four months after Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed at the outset of the U.S.-Israeli war with Iran, Tehran began a funeral procession stretching across five cities in Iran and Iraq from July 4–9, with millions of mourners expected. Iran warned Washington and Israel against attacks during the mourning period and told vessels in the Strait of Hormuz to follow Tehran-designated routes, as U.S. Central Command confirmed a second Marine Expeditionary Unit is now operating in the region.

Why it matters: Any disruption to Hormuz shipping would hit oil prices and U.S. forces are increasingly exposed in the Gulf.

Why it matters: Slowing hiring plus sticky inflation boxes in the Fed — and your mortgage, savings and 401(k) — with no easy policy answer.

Tesla crushes Q2 delivery estimates — and posts its worst stock day in a year

Tesla delivered 480,126 vehicles in the second quarter, up 25% year over year and more than 60,000 above even the most bullish Wall Street forecasts of about 420,000. Shares nonetheless sank roughly 7.5% Thursday, the third straight delivery report to trigger a selloff, as investors worried the beat came at the cost of margins and pricing ahead of July 22 earnings.

Why it matters: Wall Street is repricing Tesla from hypergrowth disruptor to mature automaker — a bellwether shift for richly valued megacaps.
Sources: CNBC, Electrek

Apple closes in on Nvidia's crown as chip stocks wobble

Apple jumped nearly 5% in Thursday's shortened session on reports of an expanded iPhone lineup, lifting its market value to roughly $4.5 trillion — about 4% below Nvidia's $4.7 trillion.
