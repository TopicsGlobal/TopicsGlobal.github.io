June payrolls rise only 57,000, sharply missing forecasts

The economy added a seasonally adjusted 57,000 jobs in June, well below the roughly 113,000–115,000 expected, and April–May payrolls were revised down by a combined 74,000. The unemployment rate fell to 4.2%, but only because labor-force participation slid 0.3 point to 61.5%, the lowest since March 2021.

Why it matters: The soft print collapsed the odds of a Fed rate hike at the July 29 meeting to about 22%, reshaping the rate outlook heading into the second half.

U.S. lets USMCA renewal deadline pass, forcing renegotiation

The Trump administration declined to extend the U.S.-Mexico-Canada Agreement by Wednesday's July 1 review deadline, starting a countdown toward the pact's expiration and a fresh round of talks. Washington wants to stop China from routing goods through Mexico and Canada and to pull more manufacturing into the U.S.; bilateral talks with Mexico are underway, while negotiations with Canada have not begun.

Why it matters: Roughly $2 trillion in annual North American trade — and supply chains for autos, agriculture and energy — now face years of negotiating uncertainty.

Fed Chair Warsh: inflation "too high," no signal on July

In his global debut at the ECB's Sintra forum, new Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh said prices remain too elevated, stressed the Fed's political independence, and — consistent with his rejection of forward guidance — declined to telegraph the July decision. He noted inflation risks have eased since the U.S.–Iran memorandum ending last month's war.

Why it matters: Warsh's inflation-first posture dims President Trump's hopes for rate cuts this year, even as Thursday's weak jobs data argues against further tightening.

Tesla posts blowout 480,126 Q2 deliveries — stock sinks ~7%

Tesla delivered 480,126 vehicles in the second quarter, up 25% year over year and far above the ~400,000 consensus. Shares nonetheless fell about 7% — the stock's worst day in nearly a year — on sell-the-news profit-taking, margin worries tied to price cuts, and China's BYD reclaiming the global EV lead with over 550,000 battery-electric deliveries.

Why it matters: Investors are now focused on whether volume growth is coming at the expense of per-vehicle profitability — an answer that arrives with earnings on July 22.
