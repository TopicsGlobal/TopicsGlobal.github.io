June jobs report badly misses: +57,000 payrolls, hike bets fade

FBI surges ~260 personnel into Georgia 2020-election probe

An FBI memo obtained by the Associated Press shows a surge of more than 200 additional personnel into Fulton County, Georgia — about 260 in total — for an investigation of the 2020 election, which President Trump has long alleged was "rigged." Democrats denounced the deployment as politicizing federal law enforcement.

Why it matters: A federal probe of a settled election, staffed at this scale, escalates the fight over DOJ/FBI independence heading into the 2026 midterms.

FOREIGN POLICY / ENERGY

U.S.–Iran ceasefire strained as Hormuz reopening talks snag on tolls

The 60-day ceasefire extension signed June 17 is holding despite recent tit-for-tat strikes, but negotiations over reopening the Strait of Hormuz have stalled: Iran wants to charge transit and insurance fees, while Oman floats a "voluntary" service fee. Thousands of seafarers remain stuck in the Gulf and shipping volumes are still far below normal, even as oil has retreated to roughly pre-war levels.

Why it matters: About a fifth of the world's oil normally transits Hormuz — the talks' outcome will set gasoline prices and the risk premium across U.S. markets.

Source: NPR / PBS News / Bloomberg
